The youngest-ever winner of the prestigious Booker Prize Eleanor Catton book The Luminaries takes inspiration from improbable match: the New Zealand gold rush of the 1860s and the zodiac. We follow Scottish lawyer Walter Moody who dreams of gold and is attempting to turn his dream into a reality through the mines of Hokitika. Walter stumbles into a strange meeting of a bizarre group, each member of the group representing a zodiac sign or an astronomical body. The group has gathered in a hotel lobby to discuss a mysterious death. Described as “It is glorious escapism, perfect for our times.”
