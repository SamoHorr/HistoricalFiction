Human Acts by Han Kang is set in the 1980s, South Korea talks about the Gwangju Uprising and its aftermath and the brave people that donated their lives for their cause. This tragic story unfolds through a series of interconnected chapters of the victims and bereaved and their echoing agony caused by the massacre. The story begins with a boy named Dong-ho as he looks for the corpse of his best friends and then is followed by six more victims of Gwangju and the people they left behind. Human Acts was an award-winning controversial novel that depicted a historic event.
