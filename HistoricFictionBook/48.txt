Thebes at War by Nobel laureate Naguib Mahfouz is Hailed as “the single most important writer in modern Arabic literature” by Newsday, talks about the triumphant defeat of Asiatic foreigners who dominated northern Egypt for two hundred years during Ancient Egyptian times. Filled with the grand class of armies, staggering defeats, daring escapes, and glorious victories written at a time when Egypt was once again under the sway of foreign powers. “Home is not where you were born; home is where all your attempts to escape cease.” “Fear does not prevent death. It prevents life”
-Naguib Mahfouz, Thebes at War
