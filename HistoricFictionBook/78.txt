An instance of the Fingerpost by Iain Pears is a compelling historical crime mystery with plot twist that keeps the readers stumped till the very end. Set in England in the 1660s, after Charles II was restored to the throne after years of civil unrest.
“He who profits by villainy, has perpetrated it.”
A fellow of New College is found dead under suspicious circumstances and a young woman is accused of his murder. We learn more about this crime through four witnesses: an Italian physician, the son of an alleged Royalist traitor, a master cryptographer, and a renowned Oxford antiquarian.
