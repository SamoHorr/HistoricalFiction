The novel War and Peace by Leo Tolstoy is usually described as one of Tolstoy’s masterpieces, a masterwork of Russian literature and even as the world’s greatest novel. Set during the Napoleonic Era in Russian society (early 19th century Russia), the novel follows the live of five aristocratic families as they navigate their ways through love, ambition, and existential questions such as youth, marriage, age, and death during an unstable and tumultuous time in their country. The novel with the help of bloody battlefields, grand ballrooms, and intimate chamber conversation manages to skillfully blend historical events with profound philosophical musings.
