Colson Whitehead famously wrote “Stolen bodies working stolen land. It was an engine that did not stop, its hungry boiler fed with blood.” In the Underground Railroad which follows Cora, a slave on a cotton plantation in Georgia. After meeting another slave named Caesar, Cora learns about the Underground Railroad, a network of secret routes and safe houses used to escape to free states. The two embark on a perilous and dangerous journey to attempt to escape to the North. This 2017 Pulitzer Prize winner manages to recreate the terrors that black people suffered through in the pre-Civil War era.
