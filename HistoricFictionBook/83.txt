Salt to the Sea by Ruta Sepetys winner for the Best Young Adult Fiction of 2016.
While the Titanic and Lusitania are well-documented disasters, but the single greatest tragedy in maritime history is little-known, the sinking German military transport ship was torpedoed and sunk by a Soviet submarine. 9,400 people died, making it the largest loss of life in a single ship sinking in history.
We learn more about this event through the perspective of four fictionalized but historically accurate voices.
“What had human beings become? Did war make us evil or just activate an evil already lurking within us?”
