In Mary Renault ambition and clever narrative, The King Must Die, Mary takes on the myth of the legendary hero Theseus and turns his story into a fast-paced thrilling tale. Renaults dives into Theseus' early years and how his formative years shape his mischievous behavior. And later on, we see how his playful personality evolved into the bravery and gallantry needed to undertake the heroic deeds and valor he displays on the battlefield. Renault presents Theseus in a refreshing new light: portraying him as a flawed human within a heroic figure, bringing a sense of realism to the mythical story.
