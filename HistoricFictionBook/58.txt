The Fraud is a historical novel written by Zadie Smith and based on the Tichborne case which was a famous legal case that captivated Victorian England throughout the 1860s and 1870s.  All of England is captivated with a trial involving Roger Tichborne the rightful heir to a baronetcy and a family fortune who was presumed dead from a shipwreck in 1854, came back from the dead as a lower-class butcher from Australia claiming to be the heir. Andrew Bogle, who grew up as a slave on a Jamaican sugar plantation, is a star witness of the celebrated case of imposture.
