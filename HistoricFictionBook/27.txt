The Twelve Rooms of the Nile by Enid Shomer is a romance novel of a charming but a made-up romance between two real life historical icons: Florence Nightingale, the mother of modern nursing and 19th century heroine and Gustave Flaubert the author of the famous Madame Bovary. Set in 1850 Egypt this book imagines a scenario where Nightingale and Flaubert bumped into each during their Egyptian treks along the Nile an unlikely friendship is forged. A passionate friendship created before these historical figures become famous for their famous world known achievements, and a friendship that alters both of their destinies.
